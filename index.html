Consider donating your spare computer power to research against COVID-19

Folding@home and Rosetta@home are programs to supply scientist the computer power needed to find new cures, they changed their focus to the recent COVID-19 virus.

These projects aim to simulate how proteins fold (to function) and predict their structure in order to study how the virus acts and design therapeutics to stop it.

These molecular simulations are expensive to compute, that's why any contribution helps.

Rosetta@home was recently used to accurately predict the atomic-scale structure of an important coronavirus protein weeks before it could be measured in the lab.

Your computer will run these simulations over long periods of time, both using your CPU and GPU, which will consume electricity and produce heat as if you were playing video-games.

You can throttle how much power is used and whether it should work when you are actively using your computer.

If you're currently heating your household, you should pre-emptively lower the target temperature by a few degrees and keep the door to the room containing the computer open.

Thank you.

Be well, stay safe, stay@home.
